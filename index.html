About

I learned chess from my dad when I was seven. I wasn't a prodigy — I lost a lot, got frustrated, and nearly quit twice. What kept me going was a coach who explained why a move worked instead of just telling me it was right.

That's still how I teach. We look at your games together. I ask questions more than I lecture. You leave with one or two concrete things to work on, not a fifty-page PDF you'll never open.

Good coaching isn't about showing off what the coach knows. It's about making the student see the board differently.

I've coached in schools, clubs, and one-on-one online for over ten years. English and Dutch both work fine for lessons.

What lessons look like

Pick one format, or mix them. Most students do a monthly private lesson plus occasional game reviews.

Private lesson 60 min · Zoom
We review a game you played recently, or work through a topic you choose — openings, endgames, calculation, whatever is actually bothering you. I send notes afterward.
Game review async
Send me a PGN or Lichess link. I record a video walkthrough with my thoughts on where the game turned. Cheaper than a live hour, good for busy weeks.
Tournament prep before events
Opening tweaks, opponent prep if you have pairings, and the boring-but-important stuff: sleep, food, what to do when you're losing. I've sat in tournament halls with students and done it remotely too.
Small groups 2–6 players
For clubs, school teams, or friends at a similar level. One themed session per month — tactics, a specific opening, endgame technique.
